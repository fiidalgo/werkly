## Core Values

### 1. Customer Obsession üéØ

**We start with the customer and work backwards.**

**In practice**:
- Ship features customers actually want
- Respond to support tickets in < 4 hours
- Get on customer calls to understand pain
- Measure success by customer outcomes, not our output

**Examples**:
- Before building, ask: "Does this solve a customer problem?"
- When in doubt, talk to a customer
- Every engineer does customer support rotation

**Anti-pattern**: Building features we think are cool but customers don't need

### 2. Bias for Action ‚ö°

**Speed matters. Decisions > Deliberation.**

**In practice**:
- Ship MVPs, iterate fast
- Make reversible decisions quickly
- Don't wait for perfect
- "Done is better than perfect"

**Examples**:
- Launch in 1 week with 80% vs 2 months with 100%
- Make the decision in 1 meeting, not 5
- Commit code daily, not weekly

**Anti-pattern**: Analysis paralysis, waiting for consensus on small decisions

### 3. High Standards üìä

**Good enough isn't good enough.**

**In practice**:
- Code reviews catch issues
- Manual testing before every deploy
- No broken features in production
- Refactor when code smells

**Examples**:
- "This works but is confusing" ‚Üí Rewrite it
- "95% of tests pass" ‚Üí Fix the 5%
- "Users can work around this bug" ‚Üí Still fix it

**Anti-pattern**: Shipping known bugs, sloppy code, "we'll fix it later"

### 4. Ownership üîë

**Act like an owner, not a renter.**

**In practice**:
- See something broken? Fix it (don't wait for someone else)
- Think long-term (2-3 years), not just this sprint
- Make decisions as if spending your own money
- Take pride in your work

**Examples**:
- Found a bug in someone else's code? You fix it
- See a process that's inefficient? Propose improvements
- Documentation outdated? Update it

**Anti-pattern**: "That's not my job", "Someone else will handle it"

### 5. Transparency ü™ü

**Default to sharing information.**

**In practice**:
- Public Slack channels > private DMs
- Share work-in-progress
- Admit mistakes openly
- Document decisions

**Examples**:
- Post standup updates in #engineering
- Share metrics dashboards company-wide
- Product roadmap is public internally
- Salary bands are transparent

**Anti-pattern**: Information hoarding, surprising people, hidden agendas

### 6. Continuous Learning üìö

**We're always getting better.**

**In practice**:
- Share learnings from mistakes
- Lunch & learns every Friday
- $2K/year learning budget
- Celebrate experiments (even failed ones)

**Examples**:
- Retrospectives after every project
- Share interesting articles in #learning
- Try new technologies in side projects
- Teach others what you learned

**Anti-pattern**: "We've always done it this way", fear of trying new things

### 7. Diversity & Inclusion üåç

**Different perspectives make us stronger.**

**In practice**:
- Hire for diversity of thought and background
- Create psychologically safe environment
- Everyone's voice matters
- Challenge biases

**Examples**:
- "Let's hear from people who haven't spoken yet"
- Diverse candidate slate for every role
- Inclusive language in all communications
- ERGs (Employee Resource Groups) supported

**Anti-pattern**: Groupthink, ignoring quiet voices, unconscious bias

## How We Work

### Meetings

**Default to async** - Only meet when necessary

**Good reasons for meeting**:
- Brainstorming
- Complex discussions
- Team bonding
- Urgent issues

**Bad reasons for meeting**:
- Status updates (use Slack)
- One-way information sharing (use Loom)
- Could be an email

**Meeting rules**:
- Start and end on time
- Agenda shared beforehand
- Notes taken (in Notion)
- Action items assigned
- Recordings available

### Documentation

**Write it down** - Don't rely on tribal knowledge

**Document**:
- Decisions and why
- Processes and how-tos
- Architecture and system design
- Incidents and resolutions

**Where**:
- Code: Comments in code
- Process: Notion wiki
- Decisions: Linear discussion or Notion
- Quick tips: Slack (but important stuff in Notion)

### Feedback

**Give feedback early and often**

**1:1s with manager**: Weekly
**Peer feedback**: Quarterly
**360 reviews**: Annually

**How to give feedback**:
- Be specific: "In yesterday's meeting, you interrupted Sarah"
- Be timely: Within 48 hours
- Be constructive: Suggest alternative approaches
- Be kind: Assume good intent

### Decision Making

**Who decides**:
- Engineering decisions: Engineers
- Product decisions: PM (with input)
- Design decisions: Designer (with input)
- Company decisions: Leadership team

**Framework**:
1. Gather input (but don't need consensus)
2. Decide (designated person)
3. Commit (even if you disagree)
4. Document decision

## Career Growth

Clear levels with expectations:
- **IC1-2**: Junior
- **IC3-4**: Mid-level
- **IC5-6**: Senior
- **IC7+**: Staff+

**Management track**: Also available (IC4+)

### Promotion Criteria

- Consistently performing at next level
- Business impact demonstrated
- Positive 360 feedback
- Manager recommendation

**Timeline**: Promotions happen during quarterly review cycles

- **Learning budget**: $2,000/year
- **Conference budget**: 1 conference/year (all expenses)
- **Internal mobility**: Encouraged to try different roles
- **Mentorship**: Matched with senior engineer/leader

## Work-Life Balance

### PTO Usage

**Minimum**: 10 days per year (use it!)
**No accrual shaming**: We want you to take breaks

### No "Hero" Culture

We don't celebrate:
- All-nighters
- Working weekends
- Skipping vacation
- Burnout

We celebrate:
- Shipping efficiently
- Sustainable pace
- Smart prioritization
- Saying no to low-value work

### Family First

- Parental leave: 16 weeks (primary), 8 weeks (secondary)
- Flexible hours for school pickup/dropoff
- Kids in background of video calls: totally fine
- Family emergencies: Always come first

## Living Our Values

### In Hiring

We hire for:
- Alignment with values
- Growth mindset
- Customer empathy
- Execution ability

### In Performance Reviews

Values are 50% of review:
- What you accomplish (50%)
- How you accomplish it (50%)

### When Values Conflict

Example: "Bias for Action" vs "High Standards"

**Answer**: Context dependent
- For experiments: Bias for action wins
- For production: High standards win
- When unsure: Discuss with manager
