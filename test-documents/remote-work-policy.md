# Remote Work Policy

## Overview

Werkly supports flexible work arrangements. This policy outlines expectations and guidelines for remote work.

## Hybrid Schedule

### Standard Schedule

**In office**: Tuesday, Wednesday (2 days)
**Remote**: Monday, Thursday, Friday (3 days)

**Core hours**: 10am - 3pm PT (must be available)
**Flexible hours**: Outside core hours, work when productive

### Exceptions

**Fully remote roles**: 
- Approved by manager
- Performance-based
- Requires 6+ months at company

**Fully in-office roles**:
- Some roles require daily presence (none currently)

## Equipment & Setup

### Company Provides

- Laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent)
- Monitor (optional, if requested)
- Keyboard, mouse, headphones
- Any required software licenses

### Home Office Stipend

**One-time**: $1,500 for furniture/setup
**Monthly**: $100 for internet and utilities

**Approved purchases**:
- Desk, ergonomic chair
- Monitor, dock
- Lighting, webcam
- Standing desk converter
- Cable management

### Internet Requirements

**Minimum speed**: 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload
**Recommended**: 100+ Mbps for video calls

**Reimbursement**: Up to $100/month

## Communication Expectations

### Availability

**During core hours** (10am - 3pm PT):
- Respond to Slack within 30 minutes
- Available for meetings
- Camera on for team meetings

**Outside core hours**:
- No expectation to respond immediately
- Set Slack status if away
- Use "Do Not Disturb" freely

## Security Requirements

### VPN Usage

**When required**:
- Accessing internal tools (database, admin panels)
- Viewing sensitive data
- Always when on public WiFi

**Setup**: IT will provide Tailscale invitation

### Password Management

**Required**: Use 1Password (company provided)
- All work passwords in 1Password
- Enable 2FA for critical accounts
- Never share passwords via Slack/email

### Device Security

- [ ] Enable FileVault (Mac) or BitLocker (Windows)
- [ ] Set strong device password
- [ ] Enable auto-lock (5 minutes idle)
- [ ] Keep OS updated
- [ ] Don't jailbreak/root device

### Public Spaces

**Cafes, airports, coworking**:
- Use VPN always
- Privacy screen recommended
- Lock device when leaving table
- Don't discuss confidential info aloud

## Meetings

### Video Call Etiquette

**Do**:
- Join on time
- Camera on for team meetings
- Mute when not speaking
- Use headphones (reduces echo)
- Good lighting (face visible)
- Professional background or blur

**Don't**:
- Multitask visibly (email during calls)
- Eat loudly on camera
- Take calls while driving
- Interrupt others

### Scheduling

**Across timezones**:
- Use world clock: worldtimebuddy.com
- Rotate meeting times fairly
- Record meetings for those who can't attend

**Meeting-free time**:
- **Mornings**: No meetings before 10am PT
- **Focus time**: Tuesday/Thursday 1-3pm (protected)
- **Fridays**: No internal meetings after 2pm

## Workspace Requirements

### Ergonomics

**Desk setup**:
- Monitor at eye level
- Keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Chair supports lower back
- Feet flat on floor

**Breaks**:
- Stand every hour
- 5 minute break every 90 minutes
- 20-20-20 rule for eyes (every 20 min, look 20 feet away for 20 sec)

### Noise & Distractions

**During meetings**:
- Quiet space with door (or noise-canceling headphones)
- Notify household members
- Mute when noisy

**For focus work**:
- Use "Do Not Disturb" on Slack
- Block calendar for focus time
- Pomodoro technique (25 min work, 5 min break)

## Performance & Accountability

### Output-Based

We measure by **results**, not hours worked.

**Success looks like**:
- Ship features on time
- High quality work
- Team collaboration
- Clear communication

**Not**:
- Being online 9-5
- Immediate Slack responses
- Working nights/weekends

### Check-ins

**Weekly 1:1s** with manager:
- Progress updates
- Blockers
- Feedback
- Career growth

**Quarterly reviews**:
- Goal setting (OKRs)
- Performance feedback
- Compensation review

## Working Abroad

### Short-term (< 30 days)

**Allowed** with manager approval:
- Called "workcations"
- Must maintain work hours (PT)
- Must have reliable internet
- Must notify team

### Long-term (> 30 days)

**Requires**:
- HR approval (tax and legal implications)
- Manager approval
- Established work location
- Country-specific requirements

**Not allowed in**:
- Countries with data restrictions
- Countries on US sanctions list

## Boundaries & Work-Life Balance

### After Hours

**No expectation to**:
- Check Slack after 6pm
- Reply to emails on weekends
- Work on holidays
- Be available 24/7

**Exception**: On-call rotations (compensated separately)

### Right to Disconnect

- Turn off notifications after hours
- Don't pressure others to respond off hours
- Schedule send emails (don't send at 11pm)

### Burnout Prevention

Signs you might be burning out:
- Working nights/weekends regularly
- Skipping PTO
- Always stressed
- Physical symptoms (headaches, insomnia)

**What to do**:
1. Talk to your manager
2. Take PTO
3. Use mental health benefits
4. Adjust workload

## Productivity Tips

### Creating Routine

- **Start ritual**: Coffee, review calendar, check priorities
- **End ritual**: Close laptop, update tomorrow's todo list
- **Boundaries**: Separate workspace from living space

### Staying Connected

When remote:
- Join daily standups
- Over-communicate progress
- Turn camera on for meetings
- Participate in social channels (#random, #pets)
- Attend team events

### Avoiding Isolation

- Schedule virtual coffee chats
- Join office days when possible
- Attend team events
- Use coworking spaces
- Connect with other remote workers

## FAQs

**Q: Can I work from a different timezone temporarily?**
A: Yes, with manager approval. Maintain some overlap with team hours.

**Q: What if my internet goes down?**
A: Use phone hotspot, go to cafe with WiFi, or take PTO if can't work.

**Q: Can I work from a different country?**
A: For < 30 days yes (with notice). For longer, requires HR approval.

**Q: Do I need to tell anyone if I'm working from home vs office?**
A: Optional but helpful. Update Slack status with location.

**Q: What if I want to be fully remote?**
A: Discuss with your manager. Usually requires 6+ months tenure.
