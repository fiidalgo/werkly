# Engineering Hiring Process

## Overview

Hiring great engineers is critical to Werkly's success. This guide covers our hiring process and what we look for.

## Our Hiring Philosophy

### Who We Hire

**We look for**:
- **Ability to execute**: Ship features, solve problems
- **Learning agility**: Pick up new tech quickly
- **Communication**: Explain complex ideas clearly
- **Culture fit**: Align with our values
- **Growth potential**: Can reach next level

**We don't require**:
- Perfect resume (we hire for potential)
- Prestigious companies (success comes from anywhere)
- CS degree (many great engineers are self-taught)
- Knowledge of our exact stack (you'll learn)

### Diversity & Inclusion

**We actively work to**:
- Diverse candidate pipeline
- Unbiased job descriptions
- Structured interviews (same questions for all)
- Diverse interview panels
- Anonymous resume reviews (future)

## Hiring Process

### Stage 1: Application

**Review criteria**:
- Can they code? (GitHub, portfolio)
- Have they shipped? (projects, companies)
- Can they communicate? (cover letter, writing)
- Relevant experience? (doesn't need to be exact match)

**Timeline**: Review within 3 business days

**Outcome**: 
- Advance to recruiter call (30%)
- Decline with feedback (70%)

### Stage 2: Recruiter Call (30 min)

**Goals**:
- Verify interest in Werkly
- Explain role and team
- Discuss compensation expectations
- Assess culture fit (high level)

**Questions**:
- Why Werkly?
- What are you looking for in next role?
- Tell me about a project you're proud of
- Compensation expectations?

**Outcome**:
- Advance to technical screen (70%)
- Decline (30%)

### Stage 3: Technical Screen (60 min)

**Format**: Live coding (collaborative, not adversarial)

**Problem**:
- Realistic (not algorithm puzzles)
- Related to our stack
- Multiple valid solutions
- Can be partially completed

**Example**:
"Build a simple API endpoint that accepts a text query, generates an embedding, and searches a list of pre-embedded documents. Return the top 3 matches."

**What we look for**:
- Problem-solving approach
- Code quality
- Communication (thinking out loud)
- Debugging skills
- Tool usage (Google, docs OK!)

**Not looking for**:
- Perfect solution
- No googling
- Memorized algorithms
- Speed (we give plenty of time)

**Outcome**:
- Advance to onsite (60%)
- Decline with feedback (40%)

### Stage 4: Onsite Interviews (4 hours)

**Format**: 4 rounds, 1 hour each

**Round 1 - System Design** (Senior+ only):
- Design a system (e.g., "Design a document processing pipeline")
- Draw architecture
- Discuss tradeoffs
- Scale considerations

**Round 2 - Coding**:
- Similar to technical screen but deeper
- More complex problem
- Focus on edge cases and error handling

**Round 3 - Team Fit**:
- Meet potential teammates
- Pair programming or code review exercise
- Cultural fit discussion
- Q&A about team/role

**Round 4 - Behavioral**:
- Past experiences
- How you handle conflict
- Learning from failure
- Values alignment

**Break**: 15 min between rounds

**Outcome**:
- Offer (40%)
- Decline with feedback (60%)

### Stage 5: Offer

**Components**:
- Base salary (competitive with market)
- Equity (stock options)
- Benefits (see Benefits guide)
- Start date

**Timeline**: Offer sent within 48 hours of onsite

**Decision deadline**: 1 week (can extend if needed)

## Interview Questions

### Coding Interview

**Example problems**:
- "Implement a text chunking function with overlap"
- "Build a simple RAG pipeline"
- "Debug this React component with performance issues"
- "Design an API for document upload with progress tracking"

**Difficulty**: Appropriate to level (junior vs senior)

### Behavioral Interview

**Tell me about a time when...**:
- You disagreed with a teammate's approach
- You had to learn a new technology quickly
- A project didn't go as planned
- You received critical feedback
- You had to make a decision with incomplete information

**What we listen for**:
- Self-awareness
- Growth mindset
- Collaboration
- Ownership
- Handling ambiguity

### Questions Candidates Ask Us

**Common questions**:
- What's the tech stack?
- What's a typical day like?
- How do you handle work-life balance?
- What's the career growth path?
- Why did you join Werkly?

**Be honest**:
- Share real challenges
- Don't oversell
- Discuss tradeoffs (startup vs big co)

## After the Interview

### Debrief (Within 24 hours)

**All interviewers meet**:
- Share scores and notes
- Discuss concerns
- Reach consensus
- Make decision

**No solo decisions**: Hiring is team sport

### Feedback to Candidate

**For those we don't move forward with**:
- Personal email (not automated)
- Specific feedback (1-2 areas)
- Encouragement
- Keep door open for future

**Example**:
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for interviewing with Werkly! While we enjoyed meeting you, we've decided not to move forward at this time.

Specifically, we're looking for someone with more experience in distributed systems and scaling APIs. Your React skills were impressive, but the role requires stronger backend expertise.

We'd love to stay in touch as your career progresses. Feel free to apply again in the future if the role fits better.

Best of luck!
Werkly Hiring Team
```

## Interview Training

### For New Interviewers

**Required training** (before first interview):
- Shadow 3 interviews
- Review rubric and questions
- Complete bias training
- Practice with HR

**Ongoing**:
- Calibration sessions
- Feedback from candidates
- Continuous improvement

### Interview Do's and Don'ts

**Do**:
- Make candidate comfortable
- Explain what you're evaluating
- Give hints if stuck (collaborative!)
- Allow use of Google/docs
- Take notes during interview
- Submit feedback within 24 hours

**Don't**:
- Ask leetcode-style algorithm puzzles
- Time-pressure unnecessarily
- Be adversarial
- Ask illegal questions (age, religion, marital status, etc.)
- Make candidate feel stupid
- Check phone during interview
